The concept

     While providing , the 4banks.net websites go beyond the goal of because each website is structured around distinct thematic lines that develop an argument to which the information in the bibliographical section of the website relates.
     We may think of these thematic lines as benchmarks: they orient the reader along a narrative that structures the data presented in the biliographical section, offering a platform for the development of coherent lines of inquiry.
     I present the formal aspect of the concept in the section on digital thought. Here we look at the substantive dimension, and specifically at the two types of benchmarks, the core and the thematic expansions: they serve the same purpose but differ in scope (breadth and length of the argument) and in origin (the authors may or may not belong to the pertinent Research Group).
     On the table of contents of each website these benchmarks are listed in section I. THE ARGUMENT.

The core

     Chapter 2. The core consists of one or more published work, typically a book. At this point, the core consists of a book I have authores: the original intent behind the websites was that they would serve as companions to these books (as indicated in the history of the project. As the project develops, it is intended that additional volumes will also be included in the core. – For an example see the pertinent section in the Critique website.
     Chapter 3. Notes develops special points connected univocally to these books. The notes refer to sections within the book, and are labeled individually for ease of reference. – For an example, see an entry in the Critique website.

Thematic expansions

     Chapter 4. Themes and Chapter 5. Monographs present parallel arguments, written by members of the Research Group the difference between the two (themes and monographs) being one of scope. – See a sample theme and a sample monograph from the Critique website.
     Monographs will be used especially for subsequent editions of each website, which will be devoted to specific topics. Each topic will be presented and highlighted in a Monograph written by members of the pertinent Research Group. The first two will be respectively on heritage conservation in the (critique website) and on structural comparison of religious systems (in the Mesopotamian religion website).
     One particular thematic expansion that is common to all websites deals, in Chapter 6. History of scholarship, with the development of critical thought in each particular discipline covered in their resepctive websites. This has a special theoretical significance, which I address in this website on a separate page.
