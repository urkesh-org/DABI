Configuration: surface structure

     The notion of surface structure refers to the formal configuration that a website takes: it is the concrete and tangible form in which it is displayed on the screen, but seen from a conceptual point of view. It is not the aesthetic aspect that is of concern here, but rather the proportional weight of the various components as they affect our perception of the website seen as a coherent whole.
     The configuration of a website deals with the way in which the display is organized and accessed as an epistemological channel. In other words, what is at stake is the way in which knowledge is conceived and presented so as to make its communication clear and effective.

Frame: deep structure

     The notion of deep structure refers to the way in which multiple formal layers are interlaced with each other. I use the notion of "frame" to refer to the nature of this

It is a complex task, which I subsume under the concept of digital discourse. It requires special attention and care, both in writing and reading a website, and once implemented it discloses the full potential of a website in light of the goal I am aiming for, i. e., the website as an indeal epistemological channel.
     It is especially in the case of deep structure that current websites fail to realize the full potential of the medium. The reason is that, while hyperlinks do effectively and efficiently interlace layers (i. e., the various pages and websites on which they call), they do not see the layers qua layers: the hyperlinks are rather anecdotal, in the sense that they draw on information whose meaning is exhausted in the fragment. It is a segmented, rather than a systemic interlacing.

Digital and electronic

     A copy of a printed page or of a photograph displayed on a screen (e.g., in a .JPG format) is digital only in a relative sense. "Digital" refers in this case to the way in which the original has been translated in digital format and is reproduced on the screen in the same configuration as the original.
     Ii is useful to retain a more restricted sense for the term "digital," to refer, namely, to its conceptual structure: it is a text that is conceived and realized with a fluidity and elasticity that is not possible in a paper format.

Para-digital

     Even an electronic version of a text or a photograph
see also under narrative
     In current parlance, the term "digital" is used in ways that reflect only in part the modalities here outlined. Of particular interest to us is the digital transfer of a paper publication in a .PDF format. This is indeed digital in the technical sense that it relies on the translation of the paper format into digits that can be assembled by the computer for a display practially identical to that of the paper version. As a result there is also the advantage of searchability which is missing in the analog dimension.
     However, all the other aspects of a digital dimension are missing, and to emphasize this difference I use the term "para-digital," in a way that is similar to that which one find, for instance, in the term "para-medic" as opposed to a properly medical doctor.
