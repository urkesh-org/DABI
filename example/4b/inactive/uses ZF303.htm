Surface and deep structure

     The structural interaction between the two modal sets may be seen, if only analogly, in terms of the correlation that was first raised in linguistics between surface and deep structure. We may say that surface structure corresponds to the electronic dimension, and deep structure to the properly digital dimension of the website structure.
     The surface structure is the one we access in a normal reading mode: we follow in a unilinear frame of mind an argument that is displayed in a way that is structurally the same as on the printed page; similarly, we search for information that is tied to the unilinear reading, much as we would a footnote in a printed text. We may say that the surface structure is electronic in nature rather than digital: the data are accessed on the computer, but not in terms of their inner digital qualities.
     The properly digital configuration of the website corresponds to the deep structure level. It goes beyond an electronic rendering because of the dynamic interlacing of the various elements. We make use of this digital modality when we work with a digital data base, which builds on an active relationality among cells that does not exist in a printed spreadsheet. A digitally structured website raises this feature to the level of the narrative, as is explained in more detail on a separate page.
