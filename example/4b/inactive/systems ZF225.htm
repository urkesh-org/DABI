Two systems

information and argument, knowledge and communication
data gathering and organization, information technology
technique/method
     We may distinguish two basic publishing systems through which knowledge is constructed and accessed: analog and digital.
     An analog system is the one, well establihed in scholarship, that is physically embodied in a paper publication. It presents the reader with a closed whole, wherein the argument and the data are laid out in static juxtaposition, with the argument flowing in a unilinear path.
     A digital system, on the other hand, presents the reader with an open aggregate, wherein the argument and the data are dynamically interacting along multilinear paths that can be accessed concurrently. I view websites as the digital correlatives of paper publications, and the 4Banks wewbsites aim at implementing this approach.
     The two systems share the same conceptual structure, and are to be seen not in opposition to each other, but rather in a relationhsip of reciprocal synergy.
