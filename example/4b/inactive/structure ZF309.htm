Structural import

     The major import of the structural makeup of the websites lies in the distinction between the Argument and the Record. Each of these two sections is subdivided into chapters that address their use in function of either the substantive (bibliographical tool) or the methodological (digital model) aims.
     While the distinction sets Argument and Record side by side, it is in fact meant to emphasize the interrelationship between the two. The Argument includes multiple discursive narratives that are in-terrelated not only topically, but also formally through extensive and systemic hyperlinks. The Record contains a full set of pertinent data which are also interrelated in content and form
     The difference vis-à-vis a linear argument linked to a set of data, whether digital or not, and whether on paper or in a digital format (such as PDF), may be described as follows The multiple narratives and the data sets are so constructed as to complement each other systemically: there are multiple layers that are parallel to each other, so that one expects the other.

Digital discourse

     The Argument/Record structure builds a larger whole, which can described as a digital discourse: it entails, precisely, a dynamic interaction among the constitutive parts of the "discourse," dynamic in the sense that the interaction is initiated within the discourse itself, and it invokes a new kind of human attention. The degree of change in the posture vis-à-vis communication is similar to what obtained when writing first came on the scene.
     It is, thus, an ambitious undertaking because it requires a new level of commitment to the process through which scholarship is constructed and communicated. The full argument rests in fact on this multiplicity of layers, and requires the development of a new kind of digital thought and of a special new posture in writing, reading and studying.
