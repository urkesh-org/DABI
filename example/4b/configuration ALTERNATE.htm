The notion of website

     I focus on the notion of a website as an ideal epistemological instrument, i. e., a channel for conveying knowledge. It is an ideal vehicle to this end first and foremost because it allows us to combine the two systems relating to data and argument in ways that are not possible in the paper format.
     Websites are so ubiquitous nowadays that it may seem pointless ot inquire about its nature and structure. But too facile an acceptance of what a website purports to be has led, in my view, to an underappreciation of its potential for scholarly communication. The present discussion is intended to bear this out.

Poetry

     The ultimate achievement of poetry is that it refuses segmentation. Yes, there may be a verse that stands out and that we carry in memory as particularly evocative. But ultimately every "piece," i.e., every word or segment of a poem, whether articulated metrically (as with verses) or not, is in absolute need of all the other pieces. That is the supreme beauty of authentic poetry, the sense of explosion one receives when everything blends into an illuminating unity.
     It is useful to mention poetry in this context because of the light it sheds on the notion of segmentation in relationship to the whole.
