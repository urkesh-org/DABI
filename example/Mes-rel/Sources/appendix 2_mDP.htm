14 uru igi-bi ní-me-lám-ḫuš-a 14 uru igibi nimelamḫuša 14 The front of the city was dread nimbus and halo,
15 bar-bé dingir-ururu-na sag nu-gá-gá-dam 15 barbe dingiruruna sag nugagadam 15 its sides the soaring, impenetrable heavens,
16 šà-bi inim-gír-KIN inim-garáš-a-ka 16 šabi inimgir-KIN inimgarašaka 16 its interior the cutting edge of a pointed dagger.
17 gišés-ad ki-bal-a ub4 sa aka-a 17 esad kibala ub sa aka 17 It was a trap to rebel regions, a pitfall, a net wielded.
18 gal-bi du11-ga-bé u4 la-ba-ni-ib-sù-ud-dè 18 galbi dugabe u labanibsudde 18 Not long was its blusterer living in it,
19 di-da inim-erím-gál nu-mu-ni-ib-bé 19 dida inimerimgal numunibbe 19 nor speaking aggressive words to start lawsuits.
25 uru nì-gig-ga nu-mu-ni-ib-dib-bé 25 uru nigigga numunibdibbe 25 - Abominations to the city -
20 šà-a-bar-a inim-si-nu-sá 20 šabara inimsinusa 20 None who dissembled,
21 inim-kár ní-kár ù nu-gar-ra 21 inimkar nikar u nugarra 21 spoke not straight,
22 LÚ.TÚG.DI nì-erím i-dutu 22 azlagdi(?) nierim i'utu 22 denied what he had said, was hostile, framed (people),
23 igi-bal nì-á-gar inim-inim-sig-du11-du11 23 igibal niagar inimsigdu (plur.?) 23 ensnared, was wicked, committed outrages;
24 KAL-na inim-búr-ra IM-×-DI.TÚG-di 24 KAL-na inimburra imdugdi(?) 24 (none) who looked (furtively) away, tricked, denounced.
26 nibruki á-bi sa-pàr-gal-la-àm 26 Nibru abi sapargallam 26 Nippur's borders formed a great net,
27 šà-ba u11-rí-inmušen-e du10 mu-un-bad-bad-du 27 šaba urine du munbaddu (plur.?) 27 inside it an eagle was spreading knees (to strike).
28 lú-erím lú-ḫul-gál šu-bi la-ba-ra-è 28 luerim luḫulgal šubi labarae 28 Aggressors and evildoers escaped not its claws.
29 uru nì-gi-na sag-e-eš rig7-ga 29 uru nigina sageš rigga 29 Justice was granted the city as a gift,
30 nì-zi nì-si-sá gil-sa-šè aka-a 30 nizi nisisa gilsaše aka 30 observance of truthfulness and righteousness, as ornament.
31 túg-tán-tán-na kar-ra gál-la 31 tugtantanna karra galla 31 (holiday)-clean clothes were upon the quay.
32 šeš-gal šeš-bàn-da dugud-de-dam nam-lú-ulù aka-dam 32 šešgal šešbanda duguddedam namlulu akadam 32 So that a younger brother honor the older brother, and he would act considerately,
33 inim-ab-ba-šè gizzal-aka-dè IM šu-a gi4-gi4-dam 33 inimabbaše gizzalakade me šua gigidam 33 that a father's word be heeded, and he make return for the reverence,
34 dumu ama-ni-ir du9-na ní-te-gá-dam nam-ab-ba sù-ù-dam 34 dumu amanir duna nitegadam namabba sudam 34 that a child humbly mind its mother, and she prolong (her) parental protection,
