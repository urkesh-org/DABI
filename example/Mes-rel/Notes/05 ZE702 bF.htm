  4. Buber and Buccellati present a similar distinction between polytheism (= objective, «It» relationships) and monotheism (= «Thou»). Buber writes: «The present arises only in virtue of the fact that the Thou becomes present. The I of the primary word I-It, that is, the I faced by no Thou, but surrounded by a multitude of ‘contents,’ has no present, only the past.» (p. 12) This description of the “I-It” relationship is similar to Buccellati’s description of a polytheistic relationship with fate. Buccellati asserts, for instance, that fate or destiny is «not a subject that acts, nor the counterpart in a personal relationship. It is instead just an inert object, which exists as the presupposition upon which one depends and with which one deals.» (When 5.3)
