 Albright, W. F. and W. L. Moran
1948 "A Re-interpretation of an Amarna Letter from Byblos (EA 82)"
in JCS 2, pp. 239-48.
     The commentary to a new transliteration and translation of EA 82 contains important grammatical observations on Canaanite Akkadian, such as the pleonastic use of the negative in coordination, fem. sing, verb agreeing with a masc. plus, subject, use of the permansive as a perfect and use of the waw consecutive.

Amiaud, A.
1887 "De la prononciation du ǝ en Assyrien"
in ZA 2, pp. 205-7.
     Suggests the possibility of reading the signs PA, WI as fa, fi, on the basis of correspondences in writing, etymology [hardly acceptable] and Greek transcriptions.

1889 "Les nombres ordinaux en Assyrien"
in JA 8/13, pp. 297-312
     The patterns used to form ordinal numbers are parūs (e.g. šalultu) and paris {šalšu), of which the first one was used for numbers referring to time division. In addition, Akk. uses also the afformatives ī (šamanū) and ay (šalšay). The last portion of the article contains comments to the section on the numerals in DELITZISCH 1889 Grammatik.
