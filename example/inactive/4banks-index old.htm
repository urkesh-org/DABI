Mesopotamia
The Land of the Four River Banks

The phrase "Four River Banks" was introduced around 2200 B.C. by Naram-Sin, king of Akakd, to refer to what we call Meospotamia. The latter is a Greek term and concept ("land between the rivers") which was alien to the ancients who lived there. "Four river banks" refers to the two banls of the Tigris and the Euphrates respectively, the "bank" being the starting point of the irrigation system which charactierized the territory from the middle course of the rives down to the Gulf. It was introduced by Naram-Sin to refer to the new political reality which his gandfather, Sargon, had started to implement and he had consolidated.
